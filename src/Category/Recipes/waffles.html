    Golden Delight: Homemade Waffle Bliss

    Time Required

    • Preparation time: approximately 10 minutes.
    • Cooking time: varies, around 5-7 minutes per waffle, depending on your waffle iron.


    Nutritional Information

    • Calories: Approximately 250-300 calories per waffle, depending on the size.
    • Carbohydrates: Around 30-40 grams.
    • Protein: Approximately 6-8 grams.
    • Fat: Approximately 10-15 grams.
    • Fiber: Approximately 1-2 grams.

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1 tablespoon baking powder
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt
    • 2 eggs
    • 1 3/4 cups milk
    • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    Instruction

    Step 1: Prepare the Ingredients

    • Preheat your waffle iron according to the manufacturer's instructions.
    • In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Mix well.

    Step 2: Mix the Liquids

    • In a separate bowl, beat the eggs until they become frothy.
    • Add the milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract to the beaten eggs. Mix until well combined.

    Step 3: Combine Dry and Wet Ingredients

    • Pour the liquid mixture into the dry ingredient mixture. Stir until all the ingredients are thoroughly combined. Be careful not to overmix; just mix until everything is incorporated.

    Step 4: Cook the Waffles

    • Pour the waffle batter onto the preheated waffle iron according to the size you desire, as per your waffle iron's instructions.
    • Close the waffle iron and cook the waffles until they are golden brown and crispy. Cooking times may vary depending on your waffle iron.

    Step 5: Serve

    • Carefully remove the waffles from the waffle iron and place them on a plate.
    • Serve the waffles with toppings of your choice, such as maple syrup, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or other toppings you prefer.

    Alternative Ingredients

    • You can add chocolate chips, fruit chunks, or chopped nuts to the batter before cooking for added flavor and texture.
    • For a healthier option, you can use whole wheat flour or almond flour instead of all-purpose flour.
    • If you have dietary restrictions, such as gluten-free, there are gluten-free flours available that can be used as a substitute for all-purpose flour.
